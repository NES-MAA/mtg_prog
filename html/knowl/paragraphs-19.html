Modeling spatial decisions with graph theory: logging roads and forest fragmentation in the Brazilian Amazon

This presentation addresses the spatial decision making of loggers and how it effects forest fragmentation in the Amazon basin. It provides a behavioral explanation for fragmentation by modeling how loggers build road networks. We will model how loggers shape their road networks by adapting graph theory to represent the spatial decision-making of loggers, and then implementing computational algorithms that build graphs interpretable as logging road networks.
