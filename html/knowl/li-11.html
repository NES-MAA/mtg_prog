Repeating Integer Paradox

The decimal representation for the fraction \(1/3\) is \(0.3333\ldots = 0.\overline{3} \) where the three dots and the bar above the 3 each indicate that the threes continue without end. This repetition of digits can be done in the other direction to create \(\ldots3333\) represented by \(\overleftarrow{3}\text{.}\) This does not look like any natural number since there are infinitely many threes. In this presentation, we use some of the same techniques used in showing the relationship between \(1/3\) and \(\overline{3}\) along with some modular ten arithmetic to give meaning to \(\overleftarrow{3}\) and other repeating integers having this form.
