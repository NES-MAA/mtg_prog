Given \(S\text{,}\) a set of \(n\) points contained in the unit square \(Q=[0,1]^2\text{,}\) let \(f(S)\) denote the area of the largest axis-parallel rectangle that does not contain any of the points of \(S\) in its interior. Further, let \(f(n)\) be the minimum value of \(f(S)\) over all sets \(S\) of \(n\) points in \(Q\text{.}\) In 2009, Dumitrescu and Jiang proved that \(f(2)=(3-\sqrt{5})/2\text{,}\) \(f(4)=1/4\text{,}\) and the following general bounds for \(f(n)\text{:}\)

\begin{gather*} \left(1.25-o(1)\right)\cdot \frac{1}{n}\le f(n)\le 4\cdot \frac{1}{n}. \end{gather*}
