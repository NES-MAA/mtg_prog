Can two games with different rules really be the same game? The classic game of Nim can be generalized to a broader category of "taking and breaking" games called octal games. A seemingly dissimilar game is Node-Kayles, which is a two player game based on coloring nodes of a graph. We investigate when, why, and how games of Node-Kayles can be octal games, and what it means for Node-Kayles on a graph to be an octal game.
