Bayesian and Measure Theoretic Extensions of Generative Adversarial Networks

Generative Adversarial Networks are a class of unsupervised machine learning algorithms that fit some probability model to a data generating process through a system of two neural networks contesting each other in a zero-sum game framework. This paper discusses extensions of classic GANs in the frameworks of Measure Theory and Bayesian statistical theory. (Advisor:Jason Hardin)
